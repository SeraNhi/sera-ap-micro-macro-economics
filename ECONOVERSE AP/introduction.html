    Recommended Study Path

    The Introduction & Foundations unit should be completed before beginning AP Microeconomics or AP Macroeconomics. These chapters establish the economic reasoning skills, vocabulary, and analytical tools used throughout the entire AP Economics curriculum.

    Step 1: Understand the AP Economics Program

    Begin with Chapter 1 to understand the structure of AP Microeconomics and AP Macroeconomics, exam expectations, scoring methods, and effective preparation strategies.


    Step 2: Learn Economic Thinking

    Master scarcity, opportunity cost, comparative advantage, and resource allocation concepts from Chapter 2.


    Step 3: Explore Economic Systems

    Study how societies organize production, distribute resources, and answer fundamental economic questions in Chapter 3.


    Step 4: Master Supply & Demand

    Complete Chapter 4 carefully because supply and demand concepts appear repeatedly throughout both AP Economics courses and AP examinations.

Success Strategies for New Economics Students

Strong habits developed during the introduction unit often determine long-term success in AP Economics.

Practice Graphs

Become comfortable drawing and interpreting graphs. Graph analysis is one of the most important AP Economics skills.

Learn Vocabulary

Economic terminology is precise. Consistent vocabulary review improves understanding and exam performance.

Use Real Examples

Connect economic concepts to current events, businesses, government policies, and everyday decisions.

Review Frequently

Short, regular review sessions are more effective than cramming before assessments.

Foundation Course Summary

Chapters 1–4 provide the framework for everything students will encounter later in AP Microeconomics and AP Macroeconomics.

Students begin by understanding the AP Economics curriculum and examination structure. They then learn the fundamental economic problem of scarcity and how opportunity cost influences decision-making.


Next, students examine different economic systems and investigate how societies answer questions concerning production, distribution, and consumption.


Finally, students study supply and demand, the foundation of market analysis and one of the most frequently tested topics on AP Economics exams.


By mastering these introductory chapters, students develop the analytical skills and conceptual understanding needed for success throughout the remainder of the EconoVerse AP curriculum.
