Course Overview

AP Economics combines two distinct but interconnected courses: AP Microeconomics and AP Macroeconomics. Together they provide students with a powerful understanding of how individuals, firms, governments, and entire economies make decisions.

```

AP Microeconomics

Learn supply and demand, elasticity, consumer behavior, production, market structures, labor markets, and government intervention.

AP Macroeconomics

Explore GDP, inflation, unemployment, fiscal policy, monetary policy, banking systems, economic growth, and international trade.

Exam Preparation

Build confidence through AP-style multiple-choice questions, FRQs, review guides, and exam strategies.

```

AP Microeconomics

Microeconomics focuses on individual decision-making and market interactions. Students investigate how consumers allocate scarce resources, how firms determine production levels, and how prices coordinate economic activity.

```

Elasticity & Consumer Choice

Understand responsiveness to price changes, utility maximization, taxation impacts, and surplus analysis.

Market Structures

Compare perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition, and oligopoly.

Market Failures

Study externalities, public goods, common resources, and government solutions.

```

AP Macroeconomics

Macroeconomics examines the performance of entire economies, including growth, inflation, employment, and policy decisions.

```

National Income

Measure economic performance through GDP, real GDP, and national accounting systems.

Aggregate Demand & Supply

Analyze business cycles, economic fluctuations, and equilibrium output.

Policy Tools

Understand how governments and central banks influence economic outcomes.

```
