AP Economics Formula Sheet

Although AP Economics emphasizes conceptual understanding, students should be familiar with key formulas and calculations. The following reference guide summarizes commonly used equations.

Concept Formula Purpose
Price Elasticity of Demand % Change in Quantity Demanded ÷ % Change in Price Measures responsiveness to price changes.
Income Elasticity % Change in Quantity ÷ % Change in Income Measures response to income changes.
Cross-Price Elasticity % Change in Quantity of Good A ÷ % Change in Price of Good B Measures relationship between goods.
Total Revenue Price × Quantity Calculates firm revenue.
Economic Profit Total Revenue − Total Cost Measures profitability.
GDP C + I + G + (X − M) Measures total economic output.
Inflation Rate ((New CPI − Old CPI) ÷ Old CPI) × 100 Measures changes in price levels.
Unemployment Rate (Unemployed ÷ Labor Force) × 100 Measures labor market conditions.

Formula Mastery Tip

Do not simply memorize formulas. Focus on understanding what each formula measures, why economists use it, and how it connects to real-world economic decisions. The AP Exam frequently tests interpretation and application rather than calculation alone.

Graph Interpretation Guide

Graph analysis is one of the most important skills in AP Economics. Students should be comfortable identifying shifts, equilibrium changes, and policy impacts.

Supply and Demand Graphs

Supply and demand graphs are foundational to AP Microeconomics. Students should identify equilibrium price and quantity, explain shifts, and evaluate market outcomes.


Cost Curves

Understand the relationships between marginal cost, average total cost, average variable cost, and average fixed cost. These curves are frequently used to analyze firm behavior.


Aggregate Demand & Aggregate Supply

The AD-AS model is central to AP Macroeconomics. Students must recognize how economic events and government policies shift aggregate demand and aggregate supply.


Money Market & Loanable Funds

These graphs help explain interest rates, monetary policy, investment decisions, and financial markets.

Graph Analysis Checklist

Use this checklist whenever analyzing an economics graph.

Step 1

Identify the graph type and the variables represented on each axis.

Step 2

Determine whether a curve shifts or whether movement occurs along a curve.

Step 3

Identify the new equilibrium and compare it with the original equilibrium.

Step 4

Explain the economic reasoning behind the change using correct terminology.
