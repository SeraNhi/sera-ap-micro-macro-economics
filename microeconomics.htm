AP Microeconomics

Master the economic principles that explain how consumers, businesses, workers, and governments make decisions. Explore markets, pricing, competition, efficiency, consumer behavior, and real-world economic systems through a rigorous AP-level curriculum.

Course Overview

AP Microeconomics focuses on the behavior of individual economic decision-makers. Students learn how consumers, producers, firms, and governments interact within markets and how those interactions determine prices, production, and resource allocation.

Microeconomics examines how scarce resources are allocated among competing uses. Individuals must make choices about how to spend their income, firms must determine what goods and services to produce, and governments must decide how to regulate markets and provide public goods.


Throughout this course, students develop analytical skills used by economists to evaluate market outcomes and policy decisions. Concepts such as opportunity cost, marginal analysis, elasticity, production costs, and market structures form the foundation of economic reasoning.


Students will also learn how economic models can be used to predict behavior, explain market outcomes, and evaluate the effectiveness of public policies. These analytical tools are valuable not only for AP exam success but also for future studies in business, finance, public policy, law, entrepreneurship, and economics.

Why Study Microeconomics?

Microeconomics provides practical insights into many of the choices people make every day. Understanding economic principles allows students to evaluate decisions more effectively and better understand the world around them.

Consumer Decisions

Learn why consumers purchase certain goods and how prices, income, and preferences influence demand.

Business Strategy

Understand how firms determine prices, maximize profits, and compete in different market structures.

Government Policy

Analyze taxation, subsidies, regulations, and policies designed to improve economic outcomes.

Market Efficiency

Explore how markets allocate resources and when market failures may require intervention.

What You Will Learn

By completing AP Microeconomics, students will develop a strong understanding of market systems, consumer behavior, firm decision-making, labor markets, market failures, and public policy analysis. These concepts serve as essential building blocks for advanced study in economics and related fields.

Learning Outcomes

Upon completing this course, students should be able to apply economic reasoning to a wide variety of situations and evaluate economic decisions using data, graphs, and analytical models.

Economic Reasoning

Apply marginal analysis, opportunity cost, and incentives to economic decision-making.

Graph Analysis

Interpret and construct AP-level economic graphs accurately.

Market Evaluation

Analyze competitive and imperfectly competitive markets.

Policy Assessment

Evaluate government policies and their economic impacts.

Data Interpretation

Use quantitative information to support economic conclusions.

AP Exam Readiness

Develop the skills necessary to succeed on both MCQs and FRQs.

Microeconomics Chapter Roadmap

The AP Microeconomics curriculum is divided into seven major units. Each chapter builds on previous concepts and prepares students for more advanced economic analysis. The following roadmap outlines the core topics, estimated study times, and AP Exam relevance for each chapter.

Chapter 5

Elasticity, Taxation, and Consumer Choice


Difficulty: Intermediate

Estimated Study Time: 6–8 Hours


Topics Covered:

  • Price Elasticity of Demand
  • Income Elasticity
  • Cross-Price Elasticity
  • Consumer Surplus
  • Producer Surplus
  • Taxes and Tariffs
  • Utility Maximization
  • Consumer Choice Theory

Students learn how consumers respond to price changes and how government policies influence market outcomes.

Chapter 6

Costs of Production


Difficulty: Intermediate

Estimated Study Time: 5–7 Hours


Topics Covered:

  • Fixed Costs
  • Variable Costs
  • Total Costs
  • Average Costs
  • Marginal Costs
  • Economic Profit
  • Accounting Profit
  • Short Run vs Long Run

Students explore how firms make production decisions and evaluate profitability.

Chapter 7

Perfect Competition


Difficulty: Advanced

Estimated Study Time: 7–9 Hours


Topics Covered:

  • Characteristics of Perfect Competition
  • Profit Maximization
  • Market Equilibrium
  • Short-Run Profit
  • Long-Run Equilibrium
  • Efficiency
  • Firm Supply Curves

Students analyze highly competitive markets and understand efficient resource allocation.

Chapter 8

Monopoly


Difficulty: Advanced

Estimated Study Time: 7–10 Hours


Topics Covered:

  • Barriers to Entry
  • Monopoly Pricing
  • Profit Maximization
  • Deadweight Loss
  • Natural Monopolies
  • Price Discrimination
  • Government Regulation

Students investigate how monopolies operate and evaluate their effects on consumers and markets.

AP Exam Connections

Many AP Exam questions require students to connect concepts across multiple chapters. Understanding these relationships is essential for success on both the multiple-choice and free-response sections.

Key AP Skills Developed

  • Graph interpretation and analysis
  • Economic reasoning using marginal analysis
  • Application of elasticity concepts
  • Consumer and producer surplus calculations
  • Cost and revenue analysis
  • Market structure comparisons
  • Policy evaluation and economic efficiency assessment
  • Written economic explanations for FRQs

The AP Exam frequently combines multiple concepts into a single question. For example, students may need to analyze how a tax affects elasticity, consumer surplus, producer surplus, and market equilibrium simultaneously.

How These Chapters Connect

Each chapter builds directly upon previous learning. Mastering foundational concepts makes advanced topics significantly easier to understand.

Chapter 5 introduces elasticity and consumer behavior, providing insight into how individuals respond to economic incentives.


Chapter 6 shifts focus to firms and production decisions, introducing cost structures and profit analysis.


Chapters 7 and 8 compare competitive and monopolistic markets, allowing students to evaluate different market outcomes and levels of efficiency.


Together, these chapters establish the analytical framework needed for later topics such as oligopoly, labor markets, and government intervention.

Additional Course Guidance

Students who approach AP Microeconomics strategically often perform significantly better on assessments and the AP Exam.

Practice Graphs Frequently

Graph analysis appears throughout nearly every AP Microeconomics unit. Consistent graph practice builds confidence and accuracy.

Focus on Cause and Effect

Economic reasoning relies heavily on understanding how one change creates multiple market outcomes.

Master Vocabulary

Precise terminology is essential for earning points on FRQs and communicating economic concepts effectively.

Think Like an Economist

Always consider incentives, trade-offs, opportunity costs, and marginal benefits when evaluating decisions.

Advanced Microeconomics Chapters

The final chapters of AP Microeconomics explore imperfect competition, labor markets, and market failures. These topics integrate concepts from earlier units and frequently appear on AP Exams.

Chapter 11

Government and Market Failure


Difficulty: Advanced

Estimated Study Time: 8–10 Hours

Recommended Study Path

Following a structured progression through the course improves understanding and retention of AP Microeconomics concepts.

Step 1: Consumer Behavior & Elasticity

Begin with Chapter 5 and develop a strong understanding of elasticity, consumer choice, and surplus analysis.


Step 2: Production & Costs

Study how firms make production decisions and analyze profit-maximizing behavior.


Step 3: Market Structures

Compare perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition, and oligopoly.


Step 4: Resource Markets

Learn how labor markets operate and how wages are determined.


Step 5: Government & Market Failure

Finish by studying externalities, public goods, and policy solutions to inefficiencies.

Final Course Summary

AP Microeconomics provides students with a powerful framework for understanding decision-making, markets, and resource allocation.

Throughout this course, students learn how consumers maximize utility, how firms maximize profits, and how markets coordinate economic activity. They explore competitive and imperfectly competitive market structures, labor markets, and the role of government intervention.


The analytical tools developed in AP Microeconomics help students evaluate policies, understand business behavior, and interpret economic events. These skills remain valuable in higher education, professional careers, and everyday decision-making.


By mastering the concepts presented in Chapters 5–11 and engaging in regular practice, students can build the confidence and expertise necessary to excel on the AP Microeconomics Exam.
